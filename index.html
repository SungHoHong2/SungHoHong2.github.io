Turning point of my life

My goal was not to work for my entire life ...
The art university seemed to provide me with that solution
Just follow the rules and you will end up tings that you do as a hobby even in a company.

I thought that was the answer, and studied hard to follow their curriculum.
At the point when I was a professional in a advertising industry, satisfying clients' demands
I begin to question am I really doing things that I do as for a hobby?

It may sound like nonsense but ...
My hobby was to pursue Art by experimenting the tools that no artists have ever used before.
I wanted to surpass the paints, brushes, and software design tools that artists have already tried countless times.
I wanted to express art in realtime with cloud computation and circuits

That's the reason why I switched to Computer Science
And as a novice in the field of Computer Science, the job market was kicking my ass ever since
Life became difficult since I have to start learning from the very beginning.
The path to working in good companies are closed since I switched my career to computer science.
I do regret sometimes, especially when I have to meet up with my friends who are already seniors in art industries

But at the same time, I understand that it is a small price to pay
to do things that I only want to do and am grateful
that I sense no distinction between work & hobby after changing my career.
Our life span is too short to do things like working for living
